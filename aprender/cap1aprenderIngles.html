...Saint-Exupéry, the author of this book, decided to write "The Little Prince" while he was in exile in the United States, after the battle of France? Exhausted from the world war 2 that was taking place at the time in his native France, and in the midst of a personal crisis and in deteriorating health, Saint-Exupéry managed to produce almost half of his most famous books in exile. acquaintances.

When the author explains the anecdote of the drawings he made in his childhood, he is not just telling us a random story. As he recounts these anecdotes, he is also beginning to establish a theme that will endure throughout the book: A critique of adulthood.

No adult who is asked what he sees in the drawing is able to see beyond the obvious. They do not use their imagination to find out what the illustration really hides. They say the first thing that comes to mind, without giving it much more thought, and what they see is a simple brown hat.
